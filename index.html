    How This Dashboard Works
    Scope: This dashboard estimates risk from disruption signals (weather, traffic, port baseline) and a stockout model. It does not directly track store shelves or real inventory levels.
    The Savannah → Atlanta route is a replenishment lane from port and distribution activity near Savannah toward grocery demand centers around Atlanta.
    The app reads live disruption signals (weather alerts, traffic events when connected, port baseline), maps them into a disruption score, and uses that score to estimate the chance the selected inventory plan runs out before the next replenishment.
    To use it: pick your current days of cover and scenario, then check whether the plan meets the target or needs more coverage. If the recommendation says 5 days and you've selected 3, your plan is 2 days short.
    Math Methodology
    1. Signal aggregation. Live weather, traffic, and port-related inputs are normalized to a 0–1 scale and combined into a disruption score, mapped into Low, Normal, High, or Extreme.
    2. Risk curve mapping. For each disruption level, the app uses an estimated relationship between days of cover and the probability of running out before replenishment. Higher disruption shifts the curve upward.
    3. Policy selection rule. Let p(d) denote the estimated probability at d days, and τ the selected risk limit. The recommendation is the smallest d in 2–6 days with p(d) ≤ τ. If none qualifies, the app reports 6d+.
    4. Comparative outputs. The interface reports selected plan, minimum recommended coverage, the gap, and a scenario comparison.
    5. Interpretation. This is decision-support, not a direct measurement of shelf inventory.
    6. Data provenance. High and Extreme stress curves at B=2, 3, and 4 match the paper's published verification table (Appendix A1, Paper 242, N=50,000, seed 42). Low, Normal, and the B=5 to B=6 extension are illustrative and pending full simulation export.
    Limitations & Assumptions
    This app does not directly observe store shelves, exact inventory levels, truck GPS feeds, or realized demand. It estimates risk from proxy disruption signals.
    Recommendations are decision support, not a guarantee of stock availability.
    Two-signal backtesting completed 2026-06-23 (ROC AUC 0.9055, 1,795-day corridor dataset). Demand calibration and traffic-signal backtesting remain future work.
