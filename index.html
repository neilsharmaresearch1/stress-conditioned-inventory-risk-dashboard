What this app does
The Savannah to Atlanta delivery route represents a replenishment route from port and distribution activity near Savannah toward grocery demand centers around Atlanta.
• It checks live disruption signals such as weather alerts.
• It estimates the chance a selected inventory plan runs out before the next replenishment.
• It recommends how many days of stock are needed to stay below the risk target.
How to use this
1. Choose how many days of stock you currently plan to hold.
2. Pick a scenario, such as baseline, safety stock, or tail mitigation.
3. Check whether the app says the plan meets the target or needs more coverage.
For example, if the current plan has 3 days of stock but the app recommends 5, the plan is 2 days short.

This app does not directly track store shelves or real grocery inventory. It estimates risk using disruption signals and a model.

How the model works
Live weather, traffic, and port-related signals are mapped into a disruption estimate. The app then uses that estimate to calculate the chance of running out of stock before replenishment arrives and recommends a safer number of stock days.
What this app does not do
Current outputs should be interpreted as model-based risk estimates, not direct observations of real stockouts. Real inventory validation and historical backtesting are planned but not connected in this prototype.
